Whispers in the Rain

The rain fell soft, a silver lace,
Upon the earth’s quiet, tender face.
And in that hush, where hearts confess,
I found your eyes, and nothing less.

Beneath the sky, so vast and gray,
We didn’t speak, but let it play—
The rhythm of the falling mist,
A symphony, in every kiss.

The world was wet with storm and grace,
But all I saw was your sweet face.
You held my hand, and time stood still,
As if the stars bent to our will.

Each drop that danced on leaf and skin
Was love’s own hymn, soft and thin—
A vow the night wrote on the air,
Of all the moments we would share.

So if it rains when you feel low,
Remember that I’ll always show—
To walk with you through storm or shine,
Because you’re forever—forever mine.
